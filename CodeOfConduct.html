CODE OF CONDUCT


     In order to provide all participants with the opportunity to benefit from IEEE events and activities, IEEE is committed to providing a friendly, safe, supportive and harassment-free environment for all congress attendees and participants, regardless of gender, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion or other group identity.

This code of conduct exists to provide information on IEEE’s expectations for all congress attendees and participants, including all members, speakers, media representatives, commentators, exhibitors, sponsors and volunteers and articulate the process of addressing violations of these expectations.
​
Expected behavior:
  • All participants are required to function in accordance with this code.
  • The congress code of conduct governs the behavior of attendees. Volunteers strictly address participants' behavior and administer the code during the congress.
  • IEEE expects conference participants to communicate professionally and constructively, whether in person or virtually, handling dissent or disagreement with courtesy, dignity and an open mind, being respectful when providing feedback, and being open to alternate points of view. Likewise, when sharing information about the organization or any attendees or participants via public communication channels, IEEE expects participants to be respectful of others  and state the facts. Do not point out other's personal lives.
  • This congress stresses peaceful resolution of conflict, equality of persons, and high moral standards of personal conduct.
  • All the attendees are expected to abide by the code of conduct and behave in a manner reflecting their professionalism.


    • Unaccepted behavior:
    • Any form of protest or demonstration that disrupts the normal functioning of the conference is prohibited.
    • Vandalism, fighting and hazing is not encouraged.
    • Harassment: Harassment includes display of sexual images in public spaces, deliberate intimidation, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of talks or other events, and unwelcome physical contact or sexual attention.
    • Sexual harassment: Requests for sexual favors and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature which creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment for another person is prohibited.
    • Disorderly behavior such as preventing guests from starting their session or intervene their addressing is not accepted.
    • Participants should not engage in inappropriate displays of affection such as kissing or long embraces.
    • Participants should not trespass through unauthorized areas.
    • Participants should not use any profane language and obscene gestures towards any other person.
    • Bullying, threatening, or intimidating should be avoided among the members of the congress.
    • Attendees should not possess any dangerous objects such as firearms, knives, guns and also illegal materials like tobacco, narcotics, drugs.
    •  The members of the conference are forbidden from smoking, consumption of alcohol and gambling of any kind during the conference.
    • Verbal abuse (name-calling ,racial or ethnic slurs or derogatory statements directed at other persons) and retaliation in any form is prohibited.

    Consequences of unacceptable behavior:

    If an attendee or participant, in either real or virtual space, engages in infringement of the code, the conference organizers will determine and carry out the appropriate course of action, including warning the offender, expulsion from the conference with no refund and/or banning the offender from future IEEE events and activities and might even handing over to the police in extreme cases.
    Conference participants seek to learn, network and enjoy themselves in the process, free from any type of harassment. Please participate responsibly and with respect for the rights of others.

    What to do?
    If you are being harassed, or notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns about an individual’s conduct, please report it immediately to any of the Student Network Members of IEEE Hyderabad Section. Immediate action will be taken accordingly.

  IEEE Hyderabad Section is one of the most active sections of IEEE in India. IEEE was formally introduced in Hyderabad, in June 1981, when a group of engineers in Hyderabad decided to form an IEEE sub-Section here. This sub-Section was rapidly elevated to a full Section, in 1984 (the year of IEEE Centenary celebrations)
